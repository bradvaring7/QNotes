3rd - Offer Solutions

Based on the diagnostic questions, offer appropriate solutions that address their specific concerns.

Key Guidelines for Solutions:

  • Asking Open Ended Questions: Do not assume a situation one way or the other in your question.
    For example, do not say: "When did the agent yell at you?"
    Instead say: "When did this happen?"
  • Active Listening: Truly listen to understand, not just to respond
  • Document Everything: Record all details for follow-up and improvement
  • Follow Through: Ensure promised actions are completed

4th - Set Expectations

Clearly communicate what happens next and when the customer can expect resolution or follow-up.

Follow-up Actions:

  • Provide specific timelines for resolution
  • Schedule follow-up calls when appropriate
  • Ensure customer has direct contact information
  • Document all commitments made to the customer

Emergency Escalation

When to immediately escalate to supervisor:

  • Customer threatens legal action
  • Safety concerns are mentioned
  • Customer requests to speak to a manager
  • Situation exceeds your authority level
  • Customer becomes verbally abusive

Remember: The goal is to turn a negative experience into a positive one while maintaining the customer relationship.
