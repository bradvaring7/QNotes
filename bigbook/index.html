Ants +
[Ant Image - Place in assets folder]

Ants are one of the most common pests that customers will frequently call in about. They are social insects living in colonies, foraging for food and sometimes causing infestations. Understanding their behavior helps identify entry points and nests, which is crucial for effective pest control. Different species may require specific treatment approaches. Regular inspections, targeted baits, and eliminating food sources are essential in managing ant infestations and addressing customer concerns.

Wasps +
[Wasp Image - Place in assets folder]

Wasps are flying insects with a slender body and a narrow waist. They can become a significant concern for families when they build nests near homes or play areas. Their presence poses stinging risks, potentially causing painful reactions and allergic responses, leading to safety and health concerns for family members.

Spiders +
[Spider Image - Place in assets folder]

Spiders are another common pests customers will call in about. Arachnids with eight legs and two body segments, distinguished from insects by lacking antennae and wings. While most spiders are harmless and beneficial, some species can cause concern for families. Venomous spiders, like the black widow or brown recluse, can pose health risks if their bites are not promptly treated, leading to anxiety and potential safety measures for families in infested areas.

Cockroaches +
[Cockroach Image - Place in assets folder]

Common Cockroaches are resilient insects with flattened bodies, long antennae, and six legs. They are considered pests due to their ability to thrive in various environments and reproduce rapidly. Cockroach infestations can negatively impact families by posing health risks, as they can contaminate food and surfaces, trigger allergies, and spread pathogens, leading to concerns about sanitation and well-being within the household.

Crickets +
[Cricket Image - Place in assets folder]

Crickets are small, winged insects known for their distinctive chirping sounds produced by rubbing their wings together. While they are generally harmless and can even be considered beneficial in some cases, their constant chirping can become a nuisance, especially when they invade homes or living spaces. For families, the noise can be disruptive and may lead to sleep disturbances, which might prompt the need for pest control measures to manage cricket populations.

Earwigs +
[Earwig Image - Place in assets folder]

Earwigs are small, elongated insects with pincers at the end of their abdomen. While they do not crawl into human ears as the name suggests, they can still be a nuisance for families. Earwigs may enter homes seeking shelter and food, potentially causing alarm and discomfort for residents. Although they are not harmful, their presence may prompt families to take preventive measures to keep them from becoming a persistent annoyance.

Cicada Killers (Form of Wasps) +
Cicada Killers are a form of wasp, so they are covered under pest control.
[Cicada Killer Image - Place in assets folder]

Cicada killers are large solitary wasps that hunt cicadas to provision their underground nests. Female cicada killers paralyze cicadas with their venom, lay eggs on them, and the hatched larvae feed on the cicadas. While their size might cause concern, cicada killers are generally not aggressive towards humans. They can occasionally disrupt lawns and gardens with their burrows, but their impact on families is minimal.

Pill Bugs/Sow Bugs +
[Pill Bug Image - Place in assets folder]

Pillbugs, and Sow bugs are very similar insects. Also known as roly-polies or woodlice, are small crustaceans that are often found in damp and dark areas of homes and gardens. While they do not pose any direct health risks to families, their presence can be bothersome and unpleasant. Pillbugs may become a nuisance when they enter homes in large numbers, leading to concerns about cleanliness and the need for pest control measures to prevent infestations.

Silverfish +
[Silverfish Image - Place in assets folder]

Silverfish are small, wingless insects with a distinctive teardrop-shaped body covered in silver-like scales. They are nocturnal creatures that prefer damp and dark environments, making bathrooms, basements, and kitchens common infestation areas. While silverfish do not pose significant health risks to humans, they can cause damage to books, paper goods, clothing, and other household items, leading to frustration and the need for pest control measures to protect belongings and maintain a pest-free home for families.

Mites +
[Mites Image - Place in assets folder]

Mites are tiny arachnids that belong to the Acari order. They can be found in various environments, including homes. While many mite species are harmless, some, like dust mites and clover mites (tiny red bugs), can cause problems for families by triggering allergies and skin irritations.

Millipedes +
[Millipede Image - Place in assets folder]

Millipedes found in American households typically range from a few centimeters to a few inches in length. While they are not dangerous to humans, their presence in homes can be unsettling for families, and they might become a nuisance when their population increases indoors. Effective pest control methods are necessary to manage millipede infestations and alleviate any concerns they may cause.

Weevil +
[Weevil Image - Place in assets folder]

Weevils are small beetles known for their elongated snouts, and they can be common household pests. They can cause:

  • Food Contamination: Weevils infest various food items, such as grains, flour, rice, pasta, and cereal, leading to contamination and spoilage.
  • Health Concerns: Consuming weevil-infested food can cause gastrointestinal discomfort and pose health risks.
  • Financial Loss: Weevils can result in food waste, leading to additional expenses for families.

Overall, weevils can disrupt food supplies, create health hazards, and cause financial and emotional stress for families affected by their presence.

Clover Mites +
These are a Bonus pest under Pest Control.
[Clover Mites Image - Place in assets folder]

Clover mites are tiny, reddish-brown arachnids that often become pests around homes and gardens. They are very small, about the size of a pinhead, and are commonly found in large numbers on sunny sides of buildings, especially near vegetation like clover and grass. While clover mites do not pose any direct health risks to humans, their presence can be bothersome to families as they may invade homes in large numbers, especially during cooler months, creating a nuisance and leaving red stains when crushed.

Centipedes +
[Centipede Image - Place in assets folder]

Centipedes are elongated arthropods with numerous legs, ranging from 15 to 177 pairs, depending on the species. They have segmented bodies and are commonly found in damp or dark areas of homes. While centipedes are generally not harmful to humans, their presence can be unsettling for families, especially if they infest living spaces or crawl on walls and floors.

Stink Bugs +
[Stink Bug Image - Place in assets folder]

Stink bugs are small, shield-shaped insects known for their distinct odor when disturbed or crushed. They can become a nuisance for families when they invade homes and gardens seeking shelter and food. While stink bugs are not harmful to humans, their presence can cause annoyance and frustration, especially during large infestations.

Boxelder Bugs +
[Boxelder Bug Image - Place in assets folder]

Boxelder bugs are small, black and reddish-brown insects that are commonly found around boxelder trees, as well as maple and ash trees. They become pests to families when they gather in large numbers on the exterior walls and around windows of homes during the fall season, seeking shelter for the winter. While boxelder bugs are not harmful to humans and do not bite or sting, their presence can be a nuisance as they may enter the house.

Lady Bugs +
[Lady Bug Image - Place in assets folder]

Ladybugs, also called ladybirds or lady beetles, are small, colorful insects with spots on their wings. They are often considered good bugs that eat plant pests like aphids, which helps keep gardens and crops healthy. While ladybugs don't harm families and are typically liked, we can go out to treat for them specifically.

Carpenter Bees +
We can treat for carpenter bees in pest control only up to 10 holes. If the customer has more, they will need a "Carpenter Bee Plan" Subscription.
[Carpenter Bee Image - Place in assets folder]

Carpenter bees are large, solitary bees often mistaken for bumblebees due to their similar appearance. They are named "carpenter" bees because they excavate nesting galleries in wooden structures, such as eaves, decks, and fences. While the male carpenter bees are not aggressive and lack stingers, the females can sting if provoked, and their nesting activities can lead to structural damage over time.

Snakes +
Snakes are "Bonus Pest" Under pest control and can be taken care of if asked by a customer.
[Snake Image - Place in assets folder]

While most snakes are non-venomous and play vital roles in controlling pest populations, some venomous species can pose a threat to families if encountered in certain regions. Fear of snakes can also cause anxiety for some family members, especially if they live in areas with a higher likelihood of encountering venomous species.

Mice +

[Mouse Image - Place in assets folder]

Mice are small rodents with pointed snouts, long tails, and round ears. They are known for their ability to adapt and reproduce quickly, making them common household pests. Mice can enter homes through small openings, seeking food, water, and shelter. Their presence can lead to contamination of food, damage to property from gnawing, and the potential spread of diseases through their droppings.

Voles +
[Vole Image - Place in assets folder]

Voles are small rodents that resemble mice but have a stouter body and a shorter, hairy tail. They are known for their extensive tunnel systems and can cause damage to lawns, gardens, and crops by feeding on plants and roots. Voles can impact families by causing damage to landscaping and gardens, leading to potential financial losses and frustration for homeowners.

Rats +
[Rat Image - Place in assets folder]

Rats are medium-sized rodents with long tails, pointed snouts, and sharp incisor teeth. They are highly adaptable and often seek shelter and food near human dwellings. Rats can negatively impact families through:

  • Health Risks: Can carry and transmit various diseases through droppings, urine, and bites
  • Contamination: Can contaminate food storage areas and surfaces
  • Structural Damage: Notorious for gnawing on materials, including electrical wires
  • Anxiety and Discomfort: Can cause significant stress and fear for families

Mosquitoes +
[Mosquito Image - Place in assets folder]

Mosquitoes are small flying insects known for their high-pitched buzzing sound. They have slender bodies with long legs and feed on the blood of humans and animals. Mosquitoes can be a nuisance to families, especially in warmer months, as their bites can cause itching and discomfort. Additionally, mosquitoes are vectors for various diseases, such as malaria, dengue fever, Zika virus, and West Nile virus, which can pose serious health risks to family members.

Ticks +
[Tick Image - Place in assets folder]

Ticks are small arachnids that feed on the blood of hosts, including humans and animals. In the United States, ticks can be found in various outdoor environments like forests and grasslands. They can transmit diseases like Lyme disease and Rocky Mountain spotted fever through their bites. These pesky critters are known to be a significant concern for families due to potential health risks.

Fleas +
[Flea Image - Place in assets folder]

Fleas are small, wingless insects that feed on the blood of mammals and birds, including humans and pets. Key characteristics:

  • Known for their jumping ability, allowing easy movement between hosts
  • Flea bites can cause itching, redness, and discomfort
  • Can transmit diseases and parasites, such as tapeworms
  • Infestations can be challenging to eradicate
  • Require thorough cleaning and treatment of pets, bedding, and living areas
