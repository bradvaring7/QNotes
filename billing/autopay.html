BILLING > Autopay

What is Autopay?

Autopay removes a step from the customer's payment process. Rather than calling in every quarterly, bi-monthly, or monthly to pay, paying online, or mailing a check, the customer can set their account to "autopay" and have money automatically withdrawn from their bank account or credit card each time they are charged for service.

Important: Always notify a customer before putting them on autopay. If a customer is not on autopay, we should always ensure that we offer autopay to customers to ease payment.

How to Set Up Autopay

  1. Go to the "Billing" tab of the customer's card
  2. Look for the gray box that currently reads "No AutoPay"
  3. Click on "No AutoPay"
  4. The customer's bank account or credit card will appear (highlighted in blue)
  5. Select the card or bank account that will be connected to the subscription
    • In most cases, this is the only card or account on file
  6. The Billing tab will update to show the selected payment method
  7. Make sure to click 'Save Billing Info' so that the change is saved

What Customer Sees

  • Before Autopay: Gray box shows "No AutoPay"
  • After Autopay: Shows selected payment method (card or bank account)
  • Customer will be automatically charged on their billing cycle

Agent Reminders

  • Always get customer consent before enabling autopay
  • Offer autopay to customers not currently enrolled
  • Verify the correct payment method is selected
  • Always click "Save Billing Info" to save changes
  • Explain the billing cycle and when charges will occur
  • Document autopay setup in account notes

Benefits to Explain to Customers

  • No need to remember payment due dates
  • Eliminates late fees from missed payments
  • Saves time - no monthly calls or online payments needed
  • Can be changed or cancelled at any time
