Disputes/Refunds

BILLING > Disputes/Refunds

This page provides an overview of what a dispute to a charge is, how disputes are handled, and specific policies regarding the sensitivity towards refunds when a customer has a dispute.

üí∏ What is a Dispute?

Have you ever seen a charge in your bank account, that you didn't understand or agree with?

A dispute is when a customer questions a charge on their credit card due to various reasons. This may include, but is not limited to billing errors, unauthorized transactions, or issues with goods or services. To start a dispute, the customer contacts their bank or credit card issuer. The bank or card issuer will send the dispute to Greenix to gather information about the transaction.

‚úÖ Good Safeguards for Disputes

Important Rule: All CX should NOT submit a refund on disputed invoices.

Exceptions

When we could submit a refund while in dispute would be:

  • Code Reds
  • To save an account in Loyalty with approval from a supervisor

Why This Policy Exists

  • If we submit a refund during an active dispute, there could be complications while the dispute is in progress
  • Could result in losing the dispute, later resulting in the customer receiving two credits on their statement
  • Fees are involved with losing a dispute, which increases the cost to Greenix
  • If the dispute is already won, then refunding that invoice requires us to send a check
  • Leaves us open to lose a secondary dispute (called arbitration) which comes with higher fees

‚ùì Preferred Options Other Than Refunding Disputed Invoice

1. Coupon

  • Add a coupon to the account for the same amount

2. Code Reds

  • If a Code Red situation, and the refund for that invoice is preferred, we can refund it once the dispute is resolved
  • Only through a check and only if we win the dispute
  • If we lose the dispute, the customer will get the money back anyway

3. Submit a Ticket

  • For all situations where a customer requests a refund for a disputed invoice, submit a ticket to the accounting team as "General Questions"
  • This alerts them to the situation
  • Also add proper notes to the account

Agent Reminders

  • Never refund disputed invoices without supervisor approval
  • Check for dispute flags and accounting notes before processing refunds
  • Use coupons as alternative to refunds when appropriate
  • Always submit tickets to accounting for disputed refund requests
  • Document all actions and conversations in account notes
  • Code Red situations may be exceptions - get supervisor approval
