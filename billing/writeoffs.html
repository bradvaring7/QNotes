You may see some accounts that have been cancelled by Greenix. When a customer is past due for their bill with us for too long (any time from 100-180 days), a member of our billing team will cancel the customer's account and write-off the balance that was left. This article goes through how to know which accounts are Write-offs, and how to properly handle cases when a customer calls in to pay their bill and may want to re-activate their account.

You Will Know This is a Write-Off Because:

  • The Account is Frozen
  • The subscription's cancellation reason will be "Unpaid Balance"
  • The amount the customer owed will be couponed out. This coupon will be labeled "Write-Off"

What if a Customer Wants to Pay Their Bill?

Even if an account has been cancelled, and a customer wants to pay their balance or the amount that was written off, you can definitely help them out with that!

1. Charge the Amount

With customer approval, review how much they owed. Even if they do not have a balance, you will need to charge them the amount that was written off on their account in order to re-activate it.

You will know how much they owed by looking at the amount that was in the write-off coupon (for example, above we would charge the customer $297.00). If the customer asks to make partial payments they can do so, but none of the steps below will be applicable until they paid their debt in full.

If the customer doesn't wish to re-activate their account, you are done there! Leave good notes on the account. If the customer wishes to re-activate their account, you can do so and go to the next step if they have followed all of the steps above.

2. Re-Activate the Account

If the customer wishes to continue services with us, if they have a payment method on file, and are willing to be on Autopay, they may continue services with us.

  • You must first get permission from a Supervisor that you can re-activate the customer's account
  • To activate, toggle "Active" in the Subscription. Make notes on the account you have done so

3. Schedule if Needed

If the customer would like to schedule an appointment, you may schedule one for them if they are due for one. If they are past due, you may also schedule one on the soonest available date.

Agent Reminders

  • Always get supervisor approval before re-activating a write-off account
  • Customer must pay the full write-off amount before re-activation
  • Document all actions taken in account notes
  • Check for payment method and autopay willingness before re-activation
